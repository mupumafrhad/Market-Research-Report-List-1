<p><p>Energy Performance Contracting (EPC) is a financial mechanism that facilitates energy efficiency improvements in buildings and facilities. Under this model, an energy service company (ESCO) undertakes energy-saving projects and guarantees a certain level of savings, which are used to finance the improvements. The contract typically spans several years, during which the ESCO monitors the implemented measures to ensure promised savings are achieved.</p><p>The EPC market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing energy costs, stringent regulations for energy efficiency, and the rising demand for sustainable building practices. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.3% during the forecast period. Key trends include the integration of advanced technologies such as IoT and AI for real-time monitoring and optimization of energy usage, and a growing focus on retrofitting existing buildings rather than new constructions. Additionally, public-private partnerships are gaining traction, allowing for collaborative efforts in executing large-scale energy efficiency projects. As organizations prioritize sustainability and cost savings, the EPC market is poised for continued expansion, contributing to both economic and environmental benefits.</p></p>
